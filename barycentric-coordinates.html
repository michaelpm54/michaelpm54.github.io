Barycentric coordinates

Published: Sun 07 February 2021
By Michael McAssey

In Notes.

The point of barycentric coordinates is to be able to express a point in the affine plane in terms of other points.

K↔(211,311,611)K \leftrightarrow ({2 \over 11}, {3 \over 11}, {6 \over 11})K↔(112​,113​,116​)

This triple of numbers are barycentric coordinates.

Can be thought of as centre of mass.

The point C can be thought of as the weighting from A to B. It is closer to A because it is weighted more heavily in favour of A.

Read the first and second lines of the Ex. carefully.

The (1−λ)A+λB(1-\lambda)A + \lambda B(1−λ)A+λB notation comes from the fact that you want a point at λ\lambdaλ on the curve/line.

Take for example 131 \over 331​. It's closer to the start than the end, as it's only 131 \over 331​ in. It's 131 \over 331​ away from 0. On the other hand, it's 232 \over 332​ away from 1.

Given a vector ABˉ\bar{AB}ABˉ, the point λ\lambdaλ is at A+λABˉA + \lambda \bar{AB}A+λABˉ on the vector. In other words, start from AAA and go along the vector λ\lambdaλ times its original length. λ=0A+0ABˉ=A \lambda = 0 \\ A + 0\bar{AB} = A λ=0A+0ABˉ=A

λ=1A+1ABˉ=A+ABˉ \lambda = 1 \\ A + 1\bar{AB} = A + \bar{AB} λ=1A+1ABˉ=A+ABˉ

If you're walking along the vector by λ\lambdaλ, to get to ABˉ\bar{AB}ABˉ you must walk an additional length of 1−λ1 - \lambda1−λ, whether it be positive or negative (if you walk too far initially, in the case that \lambda is >1).

So the remaining part segment of ABˉ\bar{AB}ABˉ is (1−λ)ABˉ(1 - \lambda) \bar{AB}(1−λ)ABˉ.

The entire length of ABˉ\bar{AB}ABˉ is: λABˉ+(1−λ)ABˉ \lambda \bar{AB} + (1 - \lambda) \bar{AB} λABˉ+(1−λ)ABˉ

Worked example:

A=(0.2,1.0)B=(1.0,1.5) A = (0.2, 1.0) \\ B = (1.0, 1.5) A=(0.2,1.0)B=(1.0,1.5)

λ=0.25ABˉ=(1−0.2,1.5−1)ABˉ=(0.8,0.5)λABˉ=A+0.25(0.8,0.5)λABˉ=A+(0.2,0.125)λABˉ=(0.2,1.0)+(0.2,0.125)λABˉ=(0.4,1.125) \lambda = 0.25 \\ \bar{AB} = (1 - 0.2, 1.5 - 1) \\ \bar{AB} = (0.8, 0.5) \\ \lambda \bar{AB} = A + 0.25 (0.8, 0.5) \\ \lambda \bar{AB} = A + (0.2, 0.125) \\ \lambda \bar{AB} = (0.2, 1.0) + (0.2, 0.125) \\ \lambda \bar{AB} = (0.4, 1.125) \\ λ=0.25ABˉ=(1−0.2,1.5−1)ABˉ=(0.8,0.5)λABˉ=A+0.25(0.8,0.5)λABˉ=A+(0.2,0.125)λABˉ=(0.2,1.0)+(0.2,0.125)λABˉ=(0.4,1.125)
